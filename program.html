NSBE

NSBE empowers students and professionals—especially Black engineers—with the skills, connections, and opportunities needed to drive innovation and shape the future of STEM. By fostering leadership, technical expertise, and industry access, NSBE helps meet the demands of a rapidly evolving, interconnected world. Committed to inclusivity and impact, it remains a hub for growth, empowerment, and global industry readiness.

The Four Main Membership Categories of NSBE

NSBE Jr.

For young students exploring STEM through programs, mentorship, and summer experiences.

Collegiate

For college students seeking professional growth, leadership training, and career opportunities.

Professionals

For working professionals to expand skills, network, and engage in industry-specific groups.

Members At-Large

For graduates, lifetime supporters, and international members passionate about STEM.

NSBE is divided into six collegiate regions. Our program operates the collegiate chapter at Minnesota State University, Mankato, which is part of NSBE’s mother region — Region IV. Region IV consists of chapters in Illinois, Indiana, Michigan, Minnesota, Ohio, Wisconsin, as well as Central Canada, Central Africa, and East Africa.

Resources

The Mankato chapter leadership committee works hard for the development and implementation of the NSBE academic retention program at Mankato Minnesota State University. It ensures that the flow of information may occur through NSBE’s chapter, regional, and national leadership by maintaining updated contact information.

Minnesota State University has established numerous facilities that collaborate closely with our NSBE Mankato chapter to help us achieve our goals. The university administration has created various departments, such as the Career Development Center, the library and research services, and the Multicultural Center, which have consistently supported and benefited our Registered Student Organization (RSO).

Our Story

Welcome to the Mankato NSBE Chapter! We are a student-run chapter of the National Society of Black Engineers, committed to empowering aspiring Black engineers, scientists, and technologists on our campus and in our community.

Our chapter provides academic support, leadership opportunities, career development, and a strong community for Black students in STEM. We host workshops, networking events, mentorship programs, and more to help members thrive inside and outside the classroom.

Our Mission

To increase the number of culturally responsible black engineers who excel academically, succeed professionally and positively impact the community.

Our Vision

To see a world where Black students are empowered to pursue engineering, thrive in academic and professional spaces, and bring engineering into the hearts of their families and communities.
